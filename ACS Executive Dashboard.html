What is the African Centre for Statistics (ACS)?

The African Centre for Statistics (ACS) is the statistical hub of the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA). Established in 2006, ACS serves as the regional coordinator of statistics in Africa, responsible for improving the production, dissemination, and use of high-quality, comparable data to support evidence-based policymaking under the UN 2030 Agenda (SDGs) and the AU Agenda 2063.

The Four Main Technical Sections: What They Do & Why It Matters

Economic, Infrastructure & Agriculture Statistics
What: GDP rebasing, national accounts, agricultural surveys, infrastructure indicators, trade statistics harmonization.
Why it matters: Provides the hard data needed to measure economic growth, structural transformation, and food security across the continent — essential for investment decisions and poverty reduction strategies.
Demographic & Social Statistics
What: Population censuses, Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS), gender statistics, migration, health and education data.
Why it matters: Supports the monitoring of human development, social inclusion, and progress towards the SDGs. Accurate demographic data ensures no one is left behind in policy planning.
Statistical Development, Data Innovation & Outreach
What: National Strategies for the Development of Statistics (NSDS), statistical training, data science campuses, mobile tech, partnerships.
Why it matters: Builds long-term statistical capacity across Africa, ensuring that countries have the skills and modern tools required to produce high-quality, sustainable statistics.
Geospatial Information Management Systems
What: African Geodetic Reference Frame (AFREF), administrative boundaries, satellite imagery integration with statistics.
Why it matters: Enables location-based decision-making for urban planning, disaster risk reduction, environmental monitoring, and resource allocation — bridging the gap between numbers and maps.

How ACS Works: Core Functions

1. Strengthening National Statistical Systems: Building capacity of National Statistical Offices (NSOs) across 54 member states through training, technical assistance, and field advisory missions.

2. Improving Data Availability and Harmonization: Promoting international standards and comparable methodologies across Africa.

3. Supporting Censuses and Surveys: Providing direct technical and material support for population censuses, agricultural censuses, and household surveys.

4. Modern Data Collection: Deploying mobile technologies, camera-based monitoring, and R programming pipelines.

5. Coordination and Advocacy: Organizing StatCom-Africa, FASDev, ASSD, and the African Statistical Coordination Committee.
